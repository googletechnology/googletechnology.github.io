Education

By Santosh Sankaran

Google is commonly known to be a philanthropic company; they foster an inclusive work environment through the Googleplex campus, which includes personal laundry, gourmet restaurants, a basketball court, pool tables, and much more. What people don’t always know is the extent that Google invests not only in reducing employee turnover, but also providing opportunities in education. Google’s extensive library of software incorporates Google Suite, Chromebooks, and Google Expeditions to deliver an affordable, intuitive interface to underprivileged students and schools.

Google Suite is the amalgamation of various software that aids peer collaboration, communication, and criticism. One such software, Google Classroom, allows students to submit work by sharing it via the cloud, a network storage that can be accessed with nothing more than an internet connection. The usage of the cloud means students do not need to be in school to turn in homework, and that teachers do not need to be in the classroom collecting homework, checking it and returning it to students. Google Classroom allocates time for teaching in the classroom without the unnecessary, monotonous duty of checking homework. Google Classroom also personalizes feedback. Remote accessibility eliminates the need to quickly check every students’ homework while still having enough time to teach a lesson. Instead, teachers can access student work at home and sift through it on their own time, which increases the amount of attention put onto the work student’s turn in. Teachers can provide feedback as they deem necessary without being pressed for time. Google Classroom’s significance is tied directly to remote accessibility, which allocates more time for actual teaching and more time for personalized feedback. These two positives work in conjunction to promote the classroom learning experience, because teachers should teach in class, not check homework.

Image Credit: Tablet News

On the topic of collaboration and cloud storage, Google Drive is a wonderful tool to utilize. Google Drive encompasses a vast array of files ranging from spreadsheets to documents to presentations, which are all stored on the cloud implying easy accessibility and collaboration. However, the benefits are not just limited to cloud storage. All of the Google Drive apps are simple and easy to use; they bear little difference from a conventional toolbar in Microsoft Word. Sharing documents no longer needs to be attached to an email and sent. One only has to click the share button and enter the recipient’s email to do so. Another great thing about Google Drive is that there is no risk of losing hours of hard work in case the computer crashes; documents autosave themselves, so one cannot lose hours of work over a trivial matter. Best of all, however, is that all Google Drive as well as Google Suite is free for schools, meaning underprivileged and underfunded schools have access to the myriad of tools that well funded schools have, bridging the disparity between the two. Installation and management is free and easy to use as well.

Another major learning utility from Google is Google Expeditions, a virtual reality simulator that allows students to explore locations across the world for half of the cost of a normal plane ticket. Students are provided the opportunity to explore places from Machu Picchu to the Hagia Sophia without having to repeatedly buy plane tickets, which creates an affordable solution to satiate the curiosity of our youth. Google Expeditions is far more interactive and intersectional than simply looking at pictures due to the virtual reality aspect; virtual reality implies that at least two of your senses are being appealed to in order to construct the imagery that one is actually at this foreign area. This is especially useful for those who learn kinesthetically, those who learn by doing. Simply reading about a place is not often enough for students, and they become bored and lose interest. By using Google Expeditions and virtual reality to preserve the interest of kinesthetic learners, educators can develop those interests and turn it into a dream or goal. This is especially important to motivate students in classrooms that have higher dropout rates and to discourage truancy. Google Expeditions fosters interest in kinesthetic learners, a great part of students, to reduce boredom in the classroom at an affordable price.

Image Credit: New York Times, Google Blog

Google’s wide range of affordable solutions are tantamount to providing solutions for all income levels. Insinuating a largely educated populace is not a distant dream nor encounter and is entirely possible due to the systematic opportunities Google provides to eliminating the ever-growing gap between the rich and the poor.
